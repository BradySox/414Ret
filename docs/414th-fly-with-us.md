# Fly With the 414th

### A campaign that fights back.

The 414th Joint Fighter Group flies its own build of DCS's dynamic campaign engine —
**414Ret** — and it turns a good single-player tool into a war worth showing up for.

You don't just fly pre-baked missions. You fly a living theater: the front line moves,
the enemy defends its bases, recon actually matters, and what you do in the cockpit
changes the map for the next sortie.

---

### What a night with us feels like

**The enemy has a plan.** Interceptors scramble to defend their bases. Fighter screens
hold the line. SAMs hide until you're committed. You earn the airspace — it isn't handed
to you.

**The battlefield is alive.** Roll in on the front and it *looks* like a war — armor
maneuvering, tracers, real firefights below you — not two frozen rows of tanks. Your
CAS run actually decides who wins the ground fight.

**Real missions, real roles.** Hunt a moving high-value target through a field of decoys.
Fly photo-recon and watch your film redraw the intel map. Crew a C-130 on an
electronic-warfare run. Run tanker, AWACS, escort, SEAD — every seat matters, and the
package needs all of them.

**Find the enemy yourself.** Targets aren't always handed to you with perfect
coordinates. Sometimes you scout, you photograph, you confirm — then you strike. It flies
like the real thing.

**A world you can build.** Want a new fight? We can start from a blank map, draw the
front, and drop in the threats — custom campaigns and scenarios, not just the stock list.

---

### Why fly with this group

- **It's always current.** Every improvement is published automatically — download once,
  always fly the latest.
- **It's actively built.** This isn't abandonware. The squadron is constantly adding,
  tuning, and fixing — a lot of it flown and signed off before it ships.
- **It's a team game.** The campaign rewards packages that work together: shooters,
  jammers, tankers, recon, and ground-pounders all pulling in the same direction.
- **You don't need to be an ace.** New pilots get a seat, a wingman, and a role from
  night one.
