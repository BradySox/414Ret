## Full version

Germany, 13 July 1988. The Warsaw Pact has already smashed across the border — Hamburg's
fallen, Copenhagen's been taken, and for a few days it looked like nothing was going to
stop them. Then the offensive ran dry. The Soviet spearhead outran its own fuel, the line
held, and now it's NATO's turn to push back.

That's the campaign we're flying with the 414th right now, and we're looking for more
pilots.

**Red Tide** is a Cold-War-gone-hot fight on the Germany map, straight out of *Red Storm
Rising* — NATO going over to the counterattack to claw back the skies and roll the Red
Army back east. Hornets, Vipers, Strike Eagles, Tomcats and Hogs against MiG-29s,
Flankers, and S-300 / SA-11 belts that reach a long way out. It's a dynamic campaign, so
the front actually moves with what we do in the air — every sortie counts toward retaking
Hamburg and freeing Copenhagen.

We fly it together as a squadron op — fighters covering the strikers, SEAD clearing the
way in, CAS working the front, everyone on the same plan instead of off doing their own
thing. You don't have to be an ace and you don't need to know the campaign tool. Pick an
airframe, grab a wingman, fly the mission.

We go once a week: **Saturdays, 8PM EST.** If '88 Central Europe in a fight that pushes
back sounds like your kind of Saturday night, come fly it with us:

**https://discord.gg/414th-jfg**
