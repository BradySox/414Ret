## Full version

Germany, 13 July 1988. The Warsaw Pact has already smashed across the border — Hamburg's
fallen, Copenhagen's been taken, and for a few days it looked like nothing was going to
stop them. Then the offensive ran dry. The Soviet spearhead outran its own fuel, the line
held, and now it's NATO's turn to push back.

That's the campaign we're flying with the 414th right now, and we're looking for more
pilots.

**Red Tide** is a Cold-War-gone-hot fight on the Germany map, straight out of *Red Storm
Rising* — NATO going over to the counterattack to claw back the skies and roll the Red
Army back east. Eagles, Tomcats, Phantoms, Hornets, Vipers and Hogs flying wing-to-wing
with the Luftwaffe, up against MiG-29s, Flankers, and S-300 / SA-11 belts that reach a
long way out. It's a dynamic campaign, so the front actually moves with what we do in the
air — every sortie counts toward retaking Hamburg and freeing Copenhagen.

And we fly it as a group, a few nights a week. Real packages: shooters, SEAD, tankers,
recon and CAS all working the same tasking order, not everyone off doing their own thing.
You don't have to be an ace and you don't need to know the campaign tool — pick an
airframe, grab a wingman, fly the mission.

If '88 Central Europe in a fight that pushes back sounds like your kind of night, come
fly it with us:

**https://discord.gg/414th-jfg**

(It's Cold War iron, so if you've got the Heatblur F-4E you'll be right at home — the
Phantom's at the heart of this one. Plenty of seats in everything else too.)
