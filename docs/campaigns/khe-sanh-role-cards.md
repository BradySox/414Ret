# Khe Sanh: Operation Niagara — Role Kneeboard Cards

*Print-and-fly quick-reference, one card per **player** role in the
[Campaign Briefing Handbook](khe-sanh-campaign-handbook.md) roster. Period **1968** loadouts and
tactics; real Vietnam callsign/role heritage where it fits. Pair with the
[mission brief](khe-sanh-campaign-handbook.md#8--the-mission-brief-template).*

> 🟢 **History & loadouts.** Tactics and ordnance are period-correct for 1968 — **dumb iron,
> napalm, rockets, and guns; no PGMs, no SAMs to dodge, no MANPADS.** The campaign **date-gates the
> loadout editor** (`restrict_weapons_by_date: true`), so the era kit below is what you'll actually
> be offered — later stores and era-wrong cockpit options are filtered automatically. Several
> airframes are **modern DCS stand-ins** for the period type (noted on each card). One meta-rule
> across every card: **every airframe you lose dents Political Will, and a captured pilot drains it
> every turn** — fly like Washington is watching.

## FAC(A) — OV-10A Bronco *(the keystone — "Covey")*

```
================  FAC(A) · OV-10A BRONCO · "COVEY"  ================
🟡 Stand-in: in early '68 the FACs flew O-1/O-2 Bird Dog; the OV-10 arrived ~Jul '68.
JOB: Find the target, mark it, run the CAS stack, keep bombs off the wire.

LOADOUT ('68): LAU Willie Pete (white phosphorus) MARKING rockets ·
               LAU-3 HE rockets · 7.62 guns · (smoke)
GAME PLAN
  • Work low-and-slow over the target area — eyes on the guns, the trenches, the armor.
  • Mark with Willie Pete; talk the attackers' eyes onto it; pass the 9-line.
    (The FAC(A) runtime also auto-marks: an airborne Bronco near enemy ground drops
     WP + calls "cleared hot" on a cadence — you and the AI both benefit.)
  • Clear each attacker HOT; near the wire it's DANGER CLOSE — they read back, you clear.
  • You are the difference between iron on a gun pit and iron in the trees.
THREAT
  • You loiter in the gun envelope — stay unpredictable, use terrain, don't orbit a Shilka.
    The flak gauntlet TIGHTENS on steady flying; keep moving and it loosens.
COMMS: own the strike freq; 9-line to attackers; "CLEARED HOT" / "ABORT". TIC discipline.
===================================================================
```

## Sandy / prop CAS — A-1H Skyraider *("Hobo" / "Spad")*

```
================  CAS · A-1H SKYRAIDER · "SANDY"/"SPAD"  ================
JOB: Loiter, hit hard, stay all day. Perimeter CAS and the rescue "Sandy" escort.

LOADOUT ('68): Mk-82 / Mk-82 Snakeye · NAPALM · LAU-3 / Zuni rockets · 4× 20mm
GAME PLAN
  • The loiter king — work under the FAC, low-and-slow accuracy, big ordnance load.
  • Perimeter CAS by day; the natural CSAR "Sandy" (see the Combat SAR card).
  • Napalm + snake on troops in the open / trench lines; guns on the gun pits.
  • Armed Recon on Route 9 is your other war — the convoys are real reinforcements;
    kill them on the road and they never reach the wire.
THREAT
  • You're in the small-arms/auto-AAA band by trade — vary the run-in, don't re-attack
    the same heading, keep moving.
COMMS: FAC check-in, "IN"/"OFF", danger-close read-back.
SAR: you are often the rescue escort — see the Combat SAR card. An AI-crewed Sandy
     auto-diverts to a live ejection; a player Sandy flies it by voice.
======================================================================
```

```
==============  JET CAS · A-4E SKYHAWK + F-100D SUPER SABRE  ==============

  Load: Mk-82/Snakeye · NAPALM · Zuni · Mk-20 ROCKEYE (vs armor/troops) · 2× 20mm
  Plan: dive deliveries from above the gun floor; the Super Gaggle's suppression jets.
F-100D SUPER SABRE (Tbilisi) — USAF fast mover
  Load: Mk-82/Snakeye · NAPALM · LAU-3 rockets · 4× 20mm
  Plan: quick CAS passes under the FAC; don't loiter in the flak.
BOTH
  • Roll in from above the 57mm ceiling, deliver, egress jinking. ONE pass when guns hot.
  • Vary IP and axis every pass — the gunners learn you fast (and the gauntlet
    mechanic literally tightens on the predictable).
  • A LOW, FAST napalm/snake pass over enemy ground triggers the snake-and-nape
    swath — the era reward for pressing the run in on the deck.
COMMS: FAC 9-line, danger-close read-back. TANKER: A-4 = probe (carrier legs); F-100 = boom KC-135.
======================================================================
```

## Arc Light — B-52H Stratofortress

```
==================  ARC LIGHT · B-52H STRATOFORTRESS  ==================
🟡 Stand-in for the period B-52D "Big Belly". The siege-breaker from altitude.
JOB: Saturate massed NVA — assembly areas, troop concentrations, the divisional rear.

LOADOUT ('68): full conventional bomb load (area saturation — the Arc Light runtime
               walks a real carpet of impacts along your run-in)
GAME PLAN
  • Box strikes on massed infantry/artillery — the single heaviest blow available.
  • Historically walked progressively CLOSER to the wire as accuracy proved out —
    but deconflict the box hard; everyone else stays clear of it.
  • High altitude, above the entire gun threat — your risk is the box, not the flak.
  • THE METER RULE: only 2 airframes, and a lost B-52 costs SEVERAL TIMES the will
    of a lost fighter. Never drag a BUFF through the 57mm envelope or inside the
    sanctuary ring. If the run-in looks dumb, it is.
COMMS: strict box/time deconfliction with the FAC and all tac air. TANKER: boom KC-135.
======================================================================
```

## BARCAP — F-8E Crusader *("MiG Master")*

```
==================  BARCAP · F-8E CRUSADER  ==================
JOB: Beat the MiG-17 ambush. Cheap insurance — air superiority is basically free here.

LOADOUT ('68): AIM-9 Sidewinder ×2-4 · 4× 20mm
GAME PLAN
  • "Last of the gunfighters" vs a guns-only MiG-17 — a fair-and-then-some matchup.
  • KNOW THE AMBUSH: the NVA fly GCI hit-and-run. They scramble LATE, slash ONE pass
    through a strike package close to its target, then run home on a short leash
    (they disengage rather than chase). Your job is to be between them and the
    strikers at the merge — kill them in the run, or let them go home.
  • DON'T CHASE into the sanctuary ring — the kill isn't worth the will penalty,
    and the leash means they're not coming back around anyway.
  • Keep energy; don't get slow-and-low in a knife fight. Use the EC-121/E-2 picture.
THREAT: the MiG-17F is GUNS ONLY (its missiles are date-gated out). Don't bleed energy.
COMMS: AWACS picture (EC-121/E-2); FOX-2 calls. TANKER: probe (carrier/Da Nang).
=============================================================
```

## TARPS recon — RF-101B Voodoo & RA-5C Vigilante

```
==============  TARPS PHOTO RECON · RF-101B + RA-5C  ==============
JOB: Film the war. The recon fog is real — what you haven't photographed, the
     campaign doesn't credit you with knowing (or killing).

LOADOUT: cameras. You are UNARMED — speed and altitude are the defense.
GAME PLAN
  • Fly the fragged TARPS run over the target; film via the F10 TARS menu on the
    pass (player-crewed), or let the AI recon bird overfly — a surviving AI run
    confirms its target automatically.
  • Film what you plan to strike (fixes the intel) and what you HAVE struck
    (turns "claimed" BDA into confirmed kills on the map).
  • Fast, high, one pass, unpredictable — the same flak rules as everyone else,
    with nothing to shoot back with.
  • A shot-down recon bird confirms NOTHING — getting home IS the mission.
COMMS: package check-in; call OFF + film state. TANKER: RF-101B = boom; RA-5C = probe.
====================================================================
```

## Airlift & lift — C-130J / UH-1H / CH-53E

```
==============  AIRLIFT / LIFT · C-130 · UH-1 · CH-53  ==============
🟡 Stand-ins: C-130J for the period C-130B/E & C-123; CH-53E for CH-53A/CH-46.
JOB: Keep Khe Sanh alive. No airlift = no garrison. And when the ring thins,
     YOU are Operation Pegasus.

C-130 — the Khe Sanh airlift (the lifeline)
  • Ammo, rations, water into a fire-swept strip. Historically: LAPES/GPES + parachute
    drops after the strip got too hot to land. Run the approach FAST, minimise ground time.
  • The strip takes real harassment fire (rocket/mortar barrages near the ramp) —
    though a field a player spawns at is never shelled that mission.
UH-1H "Dustoff" — medevac / resupply   ·   CH-53E — heavy lift
  • Hill-outpost resupply under fire — the SUPER GAGGLE runs automatically when the
    garrison is cut off: a real helo squadron flies the run with real A-4 suppression,
    and its losses are real airframes. Fly escort for it, or fly it yourself.
  • AIR ASSAULT is the campaign's territorial lever — the static front never sweeps;
    heliborne captures are how the ring actually breaks (§2 of the handbook).
THREAT: the strip + the hills are the worst gauntlet — never go without CAS/escort on call.
COMMS: Khe Sanh tower / airlift window; CAS on call; deconflict with the strike stack.
====================================================================
```

## Combat SAR — Sandy + Jolly Green *(the race for the survivor)*

```
==================  COMBAT SAR · SANDY + JOLLY GREEN  ==================
JOB: Recover downed aircrew over the NVA-held hills — and WIN THE RACE. When a pilot
ejects, enemy snatch teams spawn and go for the survivor. Beat them or kill them and
the pilot is SAVED (spared at debrief — the airframe is lost, the aviator returns).
Lose the race and the pilot is CAPTURED: a POW at an enemy airfield, DRAINING
POLITICAL WILL EVERY TURN HELD. A POW abandoned past the 4-turn clock is killed —
a CSAR raid on the field (or capturing it) frees them first.

SANDY — A-1H Skyraider (×2): find & protect the survivor, kill the snatch teams,
  suppress the guns, walk the helo in. (An AI Sandy auto-diverts to a live ejection.)
JOLLY — CH-47F pickup, with the C-130 "KING" overhead (air-tracking TACAN beacon +
  F10 survivor locator). AH-1W gunship for suppression.
THE PLAY
  • Sandy runs the on-scene picture; kill the snatch teams FIRST — they're the clock.
  • Helo ingress under Sandy cover once the area's quiet; one quick pickup.
STATUS: the rescue-scoring loop is LIVE and verified (delivered pilots are spared).
  The POW raid, the King beacon re-fly, and the Sandy auto-divert are newer — built,
  still being shaken out in-game. The NVA fly no CSAR of their own.
NOTE: invulnerable_player_pilots is ON — your own pilot won't die in the cockpit;
  the ejection → rescue-or-capture race is where the stakes live.
DOWNED-AIRMAN CONTRACT: get off the LZ-side slope to cover, guard/SAR freq,
  authenticate, let Sandy run it. (No MANPADS threat to the helos — but the guns
  are everywhere, and so are the snatch teams.)
===================================================================
```
