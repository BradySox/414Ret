# SCAR — Commander Capture & Command‑Post Intel (SME input)

Need your rulings on the specifics before I wire the campaign carryover/persistence.
Background only — the mechanics are built; this is about the *campaign rules*.

## The idea

SCAR's high‑value target rides with a **command vehicle = the enemy leader**. What
happens to him drives the campaign:

- **Killed** → clean success; threat gone, but the intel dies with him.
- **Escapes to a city** → failure (he got away).
- **Captured** (SOF team airdropped ahead of the convoy) → interrogation intel that
  **reveals enemy command posts** on later turns.

**New‑game twist:** command posts start **hidden** on the map. The only way to light
them up is to **capture commanders** — so SCAR becomes how you map the enemy command
network over a campaign.

## Already decided (your earlier calls)

- Capture **changes the next turn** (the reward carryover is ON for the commander —
  unlike a mis‑ID, which stays bragging‑rights only).
- The reward = **reveal command posts**.
- New games: command posts **hidden until first capture**.
- Capture mechanic = **SOF airdrop** (C‑130 drops a team ahead of the convoy), not an
  air‑only proxy.

## What I need you to rule on

**Reward / fog (I build this into the save first — please answer 1–3 and 7):**

1. **Reveal scope** — capturing a commander reveals… *all* enemy command posts at once,
   or just *some* (one per capture / the nearest N / only those in that region)?
2. **Permanence** — once revealed, do command posts stay revealed for the rest of the
   campaign, or can they go dark again (e.g., if you later let a commander escape, or
   after N turns)?
3. **Depth of the reveal** — does a capture reveal a command post *fully* (exact
   location + what's there), or just drop a *map marker* (location only, like the
   normal recon fog), leaving you to scout/strike for details?
7. **Is capture the ONLY key?** — should anything *else* ever reveal a command post
   (stumbling on one and destroying it, a TARPS recon pass, etc.), or is capturing a
   commander the sole way?

**SOF mechanic (the in‑mission half — follows the fog work):**

4. **Finite SOF asset** — confirm: you have **2 SOF teams**, each consumable, can't be
   reused until extracted; a botched capture spawns a **CSAR mission** next turn to get
   them back (and that team is unavailable until then). In or out for v1?
5. **Botched capture** (wrong road / missed window) — fall back to a normal kill, or
   does the commander **escape** *and* you've burned a SOF team?
6. **Scale / pacing** — roughly how many command posts on a typical map, and should one
   capture be a **big swing** (reveal a lot) or a **slow drip** (reveal a little)? This
   is what makes "reveal‑all" vs "reveal‑some" feel right.

## Why the split

#1–#3 and #7 define what capture *unlocks* and get written into the campaign save, so I
need them locked before the persistence work. #4–#6 are the SOF airdrop/extract loop and
can be built after.
