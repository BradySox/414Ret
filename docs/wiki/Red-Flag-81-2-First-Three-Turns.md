hold the FEBA, open the corridors. Copy the shape, not the specifics.*

> `red_flag_81_2.yaml` and its miz). **Callsigns, frequencies, TOTs, meter values and exact
> coordinates are illustrative** — marked *(ex.)*. The campaign is dynamic — re-plan each turn off
> the live map, the SITREP, and the campaign status ribbon. 🟡 *The campaign is unflown; the
> laydown is re-pointed at the commercial 81-2 reference miz (2026-07-02) — read exact site
> positions off the live map, the plan's logic holds.*

## Turn 1 — Map the array, hold the line

**Intent:** learn the range before you bet the wing on it. Film the SAM rings on the western
corridor, hold the FEBA, keep everyone out of the Box, and take the ambush's measure.

```
======================  RED FLAG 81-2 — MISSION BRIEF  ======================
OP / TURN / DATE: Red Flag 81-2 · Turn 1 · 26 JAN 1981
MISSION #: 001      MC: BUICK LEAD (ex.)

1. SITUATION
   The meters: WILL 100 · RESOLVE 100 (ex.) · Phase: WEEK ONE — Box 12nm +
     Tonopah ring 8nm; deep classes locked (cover page).
   Enemy: SAM rings reported Tolicha Peak + the mock-airfield complexes (unmapped);
     KS-19/ZSU flak belts on the corridors; aggressor F-5Es at TTR on GCI alert;
     armor on the FEBA.
   Friendly: FEBA holding north of Camp Mercury; Mercury/Indian Springs may draw
     harassment fire.

2. MISSION
   "The package will photo-map the western array and fly FAC-controlled CAS on
    the FEBA, in order to open Week One with a plotted threat picture."

3. EXECUTION
   a. Main effort: FILM THE ARRAY. Nobody presses a ring we haven't plotted.
   b. Scheme of maneuver:
        - SLICE 1 (RF-101B TARPS) — high-speed run: Tolicha complex + the main
          mock-airfield ring.
          One pass, film, home. Escorted at range by BUICK.
        - BUICK 1-2 (F-4E escort) — MiGCAP between the TTR axis and SLICE's track.
          The ambush comes late + close: hold at the target end.
        - NAIL 1 (OV-10 FAC) + HOG 1-2 (A-10A) — FEBA CAS: armor + gun pits
          under WP marks.
        - TRASH 1 (C-130) — Mercury resupply.
   c. Game plan vs the ARRAY: SLICE routes OUTSIDE estimated rings until filmed;
      everyone else stays on the FEBA side of the boundary. No steady medium-high
      flight west of Mercury.
   d. ROE check: all tracks ≥ 12nm from Groom; nothing near Tonopah's ring.
   e. Success: both sites filmed + FEBA held. Abort: SLICE engaged -> off-target
      egress, re-fly next turn (a dead recon bird confirms nothing).

4. COMMS (ex.): E-3 "ETHAN" 271.0 · Pkg 254.0 · NAIL 35.0 FM · Guard 243.0
5. SAR: Sandy on call; if down, win the race (handbook §11).
6. CONTINGENCIES: ambush on SLICE -> BUICK engages, SLICE unloads + extends home.
=============================================================================
```

## Turn 2 — Iron Hand: open the western corridor

**Intent:** kill the plotted Tolicha SA-2s so the strike corridor to the (still locked, but not
forever) mock airfield complex exists when Week Two releases it. Keep the FEBA fed and the
convoys dying.

```
======================  RED FLAG 81-2 — MISSION BRIEF  ======================
OP / TURN / DATE: Red Flag 81-2 · Turn 2 · 26 JAN 1981 (+1)
MISSION #: 002      MC: BUICK LEAD (ex.)

1. SITUATION
   The meters: WILL [___] · RESOLVE [___] · Phase: WEEK ONE.
   Enemy: Tolicha SA-2 + the south SA-6 PLOTTED (Turn 1 film); SA-6/SA-8 ring at
     the main mock airfield; convoy traffic on the Beatty corridor.
   Friendly: FEBA stable; film library building.

2. MISSION
   "The package will destroy the Tolicha Peak SAM complex and interdict the Beatty
    corridor convoy, in order to open the western approach."

3. EXECUTION
   a. Main effort: THE FAN SONGS AT TOLICHA. The corridor is the campaign.
   b. Scheme of maneuver:
        - BUICK 1-4 (F-4E strike, 2x 2-ship) — Iron Hand: Rockeye/iron on the two
          plotted sites, terrain-masked ingress up the western valley.
        - CADILLAC 1-2 (F-4E escort) — MiGCAP the TTR axis. Expect the slash at
          the pop point, not the push.
        - TRAIL 1-2 (A-10A Armed Recon) — the Beatty convoy (right-click the route;
          the package pre-fills).
        - NAIL 1 + HOG (OV-10 + A-10A) — FEBA CAS continues.
   c. Game plan vs the ARRAY: ingress BELOW the SA-2 envelope in the valleys; pop,
      deliver, back down. If launched on: beam, notch, unload — then re-attack from
      a NEW axis. KS-19/ZSU belts at the sites: one pass each, no admiring.
   d. ROE check: Tolicha sites are ground objects — legal. The mock airfield
      BUILDINGS stay locked until Week Two: do not shift targets onto the complex.
   e. Success: ≥1 Fan Song down + the convoy killed. Abort: undegraded ring +
      ambush merged -> egress, keep the film, come back with a better plan.

4. COMMS (ex.): as Turn 1. SAM call: "SAM, TOLICHA WEST, [clock]".
5. SAR: Sandy briefed for the western valleys — deepest tracks yet. Win the race.
6. CONTINGENCIES: the airfield-ring SAM wakes up -> it's point defense; stay out
   of its ring, it can't chase you (the SA-8 can move — treat it like a gun that
   shoots missiles). Convoy through -> expect a harder FEBA next turn.
=============================================================================
```

forms up on it — and give the escorts their rematch on your terms.

4. COMMS (ex.): as Turn 1.
5. SAR: Sandy + pickup on alert — deepest A-10 tracks yet.
6. CONTINGENCIES: Week Two arrives early (Will bled) -> the deep classes open NOW;

## After Turn 3 — where this goes

Per the [three-week plan](Red-Flag-81-2-Campaign-Briefing#7--campaign-conops--the-three-week-plan):
finish Week One with the corridors open and the arrays dying, weather the **Force on Force** push
(~turn 6 — a 2-turn red ground offensive), then spend Week Two's release on the **mock airfield
complex and the depots**. **Surge Week** (~turn 11) opens the airfields: OCA onto Tonopah Test
Range, Air Assault captures up the corridor, and the endgame — break **Regime Resolve** before
the assessment breaks you. The Box never opens; it never will.

> Re-plan every turn off the **live map, the SITREP, and the meters**. The loop: film → open the
> corridor → kill the ground → manage the arc → mass when it counts. **The aggressors are the
> test; the meters are the score.**
