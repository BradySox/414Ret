# Red Flag 81-2 — Role Kneeboard Cards

*Print-and-fly quick-reference, one card per **player** role in the
[Campaign Briefing Handbook](Red-Flag-81-2-Campaign-Briefing) roster. Period **1981** loadouts
and tactics. Pair with the
[mission brief](Red-Flag-81-2-Campaign-Briefing#8--the-mission-brief-template).*

> 🟢 **Era & loadouts.** `restrict_weapons_by_date: true` — the loadout editor enforces the 1981
> stores list (AIM-7F/AIM-9 era, dumb iron + Rockeye, no modern cueing), so the kit below is what
> you'll actually be offered. The meta-rule on every card: **every airframe is Political Will,
> every capture drains it per turn, and the Box never opens.**

## Strike — F-4E Phantom II *(the 336th TFS — the reason you're here)*

```
================  STRIKE · F-4E PHANTOM II · 336th TFS  ================
JOB: Put iron on the range targets through the array — and mass when it counts.

LOADOUT ('81): Mk-82/84 · Mk-20 ROCKEYE · era LGBs where dated · AIM-7F/AIM-9 + gun
GAME PLAN
  • The wing MASSES: when jets are available the planner fans up to four 2-ship
    sections + a forced escort onto the top target (the Alpha Strike pattern).
    Fly your section's TOT — the package only works if the TOTs do.
  • Week One the deep classes are LOCKED — your war is the array (Iron Hand),
    the armor, the FEBA. Week Two the mock airfield + depots open. Surge Week, OCA.
  • Iron Hand: the SA-2/SA-3 sites are legal ground targets from turn 1. Kill the
    Fan Song and the corridor opens for everyone behind you.
THREAT
  • The ambush comes LATE and CLOSE — at the IP, not the border. Speed at the merge,
    escort does the killing, you press or abort per the brief. Never chase into the Box.
  • SA-2 geometry: no steady medium-high flight in range; beam + notch a launch.
COMMS: package TOT discipline; SAM calls "SAM, [site], [clock]". TANKER: KC-135 boom.
======================================================================
```

## MiGCAP / Escort — F-4E Phantom II

```
================  MiGCAP · F-4E PHANTOM II  ================
JOB: Keep the aggressors off the strikers. This is the fight Red Flag was built for.

LOADOUT ('81): AIM-7F ×4 · AIM-9 ×4 · the gun. It's a VISUAL arena — the era doctrine
               fights at knife range (~22 NM commits, merges in sight).
GAME PLAN
  • KNOW THE AMBUSH: GCI holds them low and quiet; they commit LATE and slash the
    package CLOSE to its target. Your station is between the ambush axis
    (TTR / the Box) and the strikers — at the target end, not the push point.
  • They hit once and leash home. Kill them in the run; do NOT chase a bugging
    F-5 toward Groom — the Box violation costs more than the kill scores.
  • F-5E is tiny and hard to see: radar sort early, visual lookout always,
    two-ship mutual support, fight in the vertical (they'll knife-fight flat).
THREAT: hand-picked High-skill aggressors. The airframe is their only handicap.
COMMS: E-3 picture ("ETHAN" ex.); merge calls; FOX + kill removal discipline.
=============================================================
```

## BAI / Armed Recon — A-10A Thunderbolt II *(new to the flag)*

```
==============  BAI · A-10A THUNDERBOLT II  ==============
JOB: The FEBA, the armor arrays, and the convoy war on the range roads.

LOADOUT ('81): Mk-82 · Mk-20 ROCKEYE · the GAU-8 · AIM-9 self-defense
GAME PLAN
  • Kawich Valley + Gold Flat tank arrays and the FEBA armor are your target set —
    legal from turn 1, FAC-marked when the Bronco's up.
  • TRAIL WAR: right-click the enemy supply route on the map — the package
    pre-fills Armed Recon. The convoys are red's real reinforcements; kill them
    on the road and the FEBA never gets them. Decisive all campaign.
  • You live in the gun envelope by trade: the gauntlet TIGHTENS on steady flying.
    Vary the run-in, use the terrain, one pass per axis.
THREAT
  • ZSU belts on every corridor; the SA-2 rings overlap some of your ground —
    know where the sites are (TARPS film) and stay under/around the envelope.
  • An F-5 slash can reach you — keep a lookout, be ready to spit one out in a turn.
COMMS: FAC 9-line when marked; GUNS calls with clock + type. TANKER: boom (rarely needed).
====================================================================
```

====================================================================

## Combat SAR — Sandy + pickup *(the race, desert edition)*

```
==================  COMBAT SAR · THE RACE  ==================
JOB: When a pilot ejects over the ranges, the Red Force snatch teams spawn and
RACE you to the survivor. Win: the pilot is spared at debrief (airframe still
lost). Lose: CAPTURED — a POW at a red field, draining Political Will EVERY
turn held; freed by a CSAR raid or a field capture; killed past the 4-turn clock.

THE PACKAGE: Sandy escort (an AI Sandy auto-diverts to a live ejection) +
  helo pickup + the C-130 KING overhead (TACAN + F10 locator).
THE PLAY: kill the snatch teams FIRST — they are the clock. Then the pickup.
STATUS: rescue scoring live/verified fork-wide; POW raid + King beacon newer,
  still being shaken out. auto_combat_sar (OFF by default) stands up an AI alert.
NOTE: invulnerable_player_pilots is ON — the stakes live in the ejection race.
=============================================================
```
